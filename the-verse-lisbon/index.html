Verdict & Recommended Action Plan

Strengths

✅ Rare Combination of Quality Signals

NYT + Condé Nast editorial in 15 months is genuinely exceptional — editorial teams rarely cover <15-room properties unless the product is world-class. 9.7 Booking score + Mr & Mrs Smith listing = product quality validated externally. Freehold unencumbered = full strategic flexibility.

Key Risk

⚠ Why Is It For Sale After 15 Months?

This is the only question that matters before NDA. 15 months post-opening is too early for a strategic exit unless: (a) founder needs capital for next project, or (b) there's an undisclosed operational or financial issue. Due diligence must resolve this completely.

Financial Target

📋 Entry: €4.5–5.0M · Exit: €8M+ (10Y)

At €5M entry, unlevered IRR ≈ 17% over 10 years with optimization. With leverage (50–60% LTV, EUR rate ~4.5%), equity IRR increases to 22–28%. Breakeven occupancy at base EBITDA: ~55%.

RCC Strategic Fit

🌍 European Flagship + Brand Platform

Acquiring The Verse gives RCC a European editorial platform to amplify Vietnam hospitality projects globally. The NYT/Condé Nast relationship = credibility asset that travels. Lisbon's growing Asian visitor market (China +14%) is natural demand alignment for RCC's network.

Bottom Line

The Verse is a scarce, high-quality asset in a supply-constrained prime location. The editorial validation in 15 months is genuinely exceptional. However, the asking price of €15M (€1M/key, 4.7% cap rate) is 3× fair market value and financially unjustifiable. The owner's motivation (capital needed for another time-sensitive project) is benign and creates a real negotiation window. Strategy: Open at €10M, target close at €12–12.5M, max stretch €13.5M. With EBITDA at €1M+/year and a prime freehold unencumbered asset, this is a quality deal at the right price. Lead with a 30-day all-cash close — the seller's time pressure is your negotiating advantage. Walk away above €16M.
